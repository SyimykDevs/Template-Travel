  • Our mountains and our location

    Kyrgyzstan is a landlocked country in Central Asia. The national territory extends approximately 925 km from east to west and 454 km from north to south. Kyrgyzstan is bordered to the east and southeast by China, to the north by Kazakhstan, to the west by Uzbekistan and to the south by Tajikistan. The borders with Uzbekistan and Tajikistan in the Fergana Valley are quite difficult to cross. One consequence of Stalin's division of Central Asia into five republics is that many ethnic Kyrgyz do not live in Kyrgyzstan. Three enclaves were created, which are legally part of the territory of Kyrgyzstan, but geographically separated by several kilometers: two in Uzbekistan and one in Tajikistan. The topography of Kyrgyzstan is dominated by the Tien Shan and Pamir mountain systems, which together occupy about 65% of the national territory. Part of the Alai Range dominates the country's southwestern crescent, while to the east the main Tien Shan range runs along the border between southern Kyrgyzstan and China before extending further east into China's Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region. The average altitude of the territory of Kyrgyzstan is 2750 meters, ranging from 7439 m at Pobeda Peak to 394 meters in the Fergana Valley near the city of Osh. Almost 90% of the country's territory is located more than 1,500 meters above sea level.

  • About our treks and trips

    Hello, dear tourists and travelers! Our company "Alpins" is delighted to invite you to explore the wonders of our beloved Kyrgyzstan. Within the framework of our tour, you will have the opportunity to see the most beautiful that our homeland offers: famous landmarks, picturesque forests, lakes, clean air, and also listen to interesting stories about our ancestors.

    We offer various hiking and excursion options for you to choose from, so everyone can find the one that will be the most interesting and useful. Here are a few examples:

    • A hike to the Mount Toru (7301 m), the highest peak in Kyrgyzstan, and one of the highest peaks on the Silk Road.
    • An excursion to the Karakol Valley, famous for its golden caves, bronze-age cave paintings, and artificial lakes.
    • A hike to the Issyk-Kul Dam, the world's highest dam, and an assessment of the ecosystem of this massive reservoir.
    • An excursion to the Manas Mountain, a sacred place for Kyrgyz people, where the ashes of the founder and the epic-lyric poet Manas are located.

    In addition to active leisure activities, our tour also includes the opportunity for solitude in teahouses, a visit to the master workshops, and an immersion in the traditional culture of Kyrgyzstan. You can experience a sense of pilgrimage at any time during your tour.
